Counterfactual Crash Generation

Abstract

Video diffusion techniques have advanced significantly in recent years; however, they struggle to generate realistic car crashes due to the scarcity of accident events in most driving datasets. Given the high stakes involved in traffic accidents and their impact on human lives, we propose Ctrl-Crash to tackle the challenge of controllable car crash video generation by conditioning a diffusion model on signals such as bounding boxes, crash types, and the initial image frame. Our approach enables counterfactual scenario generation where minor variations in input can lead to dramatically different crash outcomes. To support fine-grained control at inference time, we leverage classifier-free guidance with independently tunable scales for each conditioning signal. Ctrl-Crash achieves superior performance across quantitative metrics (e.g., FVD) and qualitative visual quality compared to prior diffusion-based methods, marking a step toward more realistic and actionable driving video synthesis for autonomous vehicle simulation, training, and safety analysis.

Crash Prediction

We evaluate our approach using simulated intersection scenarios on NuScene data, where the ego car (green) remains stationary. In the bird's eye view map, we manually place new agents (red) crossing the intersection from left to right or right to left. These agents' bounding boxes are then projected onto the ego car's perspective. The resulting annotated data is used as input for the Box2Video network, which generates realistic video sequences.
